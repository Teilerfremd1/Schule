- 2014: Scottish Independence failed closely

## The political system of the UK
#### The government consists of...
- the Prime Minister (PM) $\Rightarrow$ Keir Starmer (Labour)
	- Leader of the strongest party in the House of Commons
- the Cabinet ($\approx$ 20 Ministers)
#### The monarch/sovereign
- head of the state with only **representational** functions and **formal** duties, e.g. **opening Parliament**
- after a general election officially appoints **PM**
- **may not enter the House of Commons**
- meets with **PM** on a regular basis
- approves Bills

#### Parliament consists of
- The **House of Commons**, the first parliamentary chamber
	- **supreme legislative** authority for the UK
	- general elections at least every 5 years
	- 650 Members of Parliament (MPs); one for each constituency (=Wahlkreis)
	- **introduces new bills**
- The **House of Lords**, the second parliamentary chamber
	- at the moment $\approx$ 800 Members
	- types of members: e.g. Angelican Bishops, life peers
	- heritary peerage was abolished in 1999
	- can **suggest** changes to bills and **delay** them for max. one year
